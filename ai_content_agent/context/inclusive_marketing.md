# IvyEdge Inclusive Marketing Framework

Every post, social caption, video script, and visual direction must pass this checklist
before publishing. Apply it at every phase of content creation — not just as a final review.

---

## Accurate and Reflects Reality
**Compliant:** Language is consciously chosen to avoid reinforcing stereotypes. Acknowledges
the diversity of human identities. Uses correct pronouns. Does not assume a gender binary.

**Non-compliant:** Reinforces stereotypes, oversimplifies human identities, adheres to a
gender binary, or fails to acknowledge the impact of automatic biases.

## Respectful Communication
**Compliant:** Correct spelling and use of names for individuals or groups. No assumptions
about cultural background in written or visual content.

**Non-compliant:** Misusing or abbreviating cultural names. Using colloquial language that
may be disrespectful to certain groups.

## Inclusivity and Engagement
**Compliant:** Uses inclusive language ("all are welcome"). Respects preferred terms for
communities (e.g., "Indigenous" not "Native American").

**Non-compliant:** Exclusionary language or phrases that might alienate certain groups.

## Perspective Incorporation
**Compliant:** Actively incorporates a variety of identities, experiences, and perspectives.
Acknowledges and values differences within the audience.

**Non-compliant:** Assumes a uniform audience background. Fails to acknowledge or represent
the variety of identities and experiences in society.

## Prevention of Erasure
**Compliant:** Acknowledges the complexities of race, gender, economic status, etc. and
their impact. Content is well-researched and informed.

**Non-compliant:** Lacks diverse perspective. Not informed by research. Makes assumptions
about groups.

## Sensitivity to Pain Points
**Compliant:** Mindful of historical and cultural sensitivities. Avoids terms that
trivialize mental health or traumatic events.

**Non-compliant:** Uses idioms or references that might be culturally insensitive or
triggering.

## Focus on Shared Progress
**Compliant:** Communicates progress that has happened to date. Highlights positive
outcomes when action is taken. Focuses on a desirable future state that includes all groups.

**Non-compliant:** Assigns blame or "points fingers." Focuses the narrative solely on the
past without looking forward. Focuses only on the undesirable current state.

---

## How to apply this at IvyEdge

IvyEdge serves women across a wide range of backgrounds — freelancers, career returners,
established entrepreneurs, corporate climbers. Our four personas (Priya, Maya, Carmen,
Dominique) reflect different ethnicities, life stages, income types, and paths. Content
must feel written for all of them, not just one.

**Specific checks for every piece:**
- Are we representing the full range of our personas, not just the most "universal"?
- Are financial examples grounded in real scenarios that reflect diverse experiences?
- Are we describing systemic problems (the financial system's failures) without assigning
  blame to individuals or making any group feel singled out?
- Are visuals and imagery directions inclusive across race, body type, age, and context?
- Are we using language that acknowledges the emotional and financial reality of our
  readers without being patronizing or reductive?
- Are CTAs framed as invitations, not demands?
